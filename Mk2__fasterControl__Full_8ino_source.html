1/* Mk2_fasterControl_twoLoads_5.ino
2 *
3 * (initially released as Mk2_bothDisplays_1 in March 2014)
4 * This sketch is for diverting surplus PV power to a dump load using a triac or
5 * Solid State Relay. It is based on the Mk2i PV Router code that I have posted in on
6 * the OpenEnergyMonitor forum. The original version, and other related material,
7 * can be found on my Summary Page at www.openenergymonitor.org/emon/node/1757
8 *
9 * In this latest version, the pin-allocations have been changed to suit my
10 * PCB-based hardware for the Mk2 PV Router. The integral voltage sensor is
11 * fed from one of the secondary coils of the transformer. Current is measured
12 * via Current Transformers at the CT1 and CT1 ports.
13 *
14 * CT1 is for 'grid' current, to be measured at the grid supply point.
15 * CT2 is for the load current, so that diverted energy can be recorded
16 *
17 * A persistence-based 4-digit display is supported. This can be driven in two
18 * different ways, one with an extra pair of logic chips, and one without. The
19 * appropriate version of the sketch must be selected by including or commenting
20 * out the "#define PIN_SAVING_HARDWARE" statement near the top of the code.
21 *
22 * September 2014: renamed as Mk2_bothDisplays_2, with these changes:
23 * - cycleCount removed (was not actually used in this sketch, but could have overflowed);
24 * - removal of unhelpful comments in the IO pin section;
25 * - tidier initialisation of display logic in setup();
26 * - addition of REQUIRED_EXPORT_IN_WATTS logic (useful as a built-in PV simulation facility);
27 *
28 * December 2014: renamed as Mk2_bothDisplays_3, with these changes:
29 * - persistence check added for zero-crossing detection (polarityConfirmed)
30 * - lowestNoOfSampleSetsPerMainsCycle added, to check for any disturbances
31 *
32 * December 2014: renamed as Mk2_bothDisplays_3a, with some typographical errors fixed.
33 *
34 * January 2016: renamed as Mk2_bothDisplays_3b, with a minor change in the ISR to
35 * remove a timing uncertainty.
36 *
37 * January 2016: updated to Mk2_bothDisplays_3c:
38 * The variables to store the ADC results are now declared as "volatile" to remove
39 * any possibility of incorrect operation due to optimisation by the compiler.
40 *
41 * February 2016: updated to Mk2_bothDisplays_4, with these changes:
42 * - improvements to the start-up logic. The start of normal operation is now
43 * synchronized with the start of a new mains cycle.
44 * - reduce the amount of feedback in the Low Pass Filter for removing the DC content
45 * from the Vsample stream. This resolves an anomaly which has been present since
46 * the start of this project. Although the amount of feedback has previously been
47 * excessive, this anomaly has had minimal effect on the system's overall behaviour.
48 * - removal of the unhelpful "triggerNeedsToBeArmed" mechanism
49 * - tidying of the "confirmPolarity" logic to make its behaviour more clear
50 * - SWEETZONE_IN_JOULES changed to WORKING_ZONE_IN_JOULES
51 * - change "triac" to "load" wherever appropriate
52 *
53 * November 2019: updated to Mk2_fasterControl_1 with these changes:
54 * - Half way through each mains cycle, a prediction is made of the likely energy level at the
55 * end of the cycle. That predicted value allows the triac to be switched at the +ve going
56 * zero-crossing point rather than waiting for a further 10 ms. These changes allow for
57 * faster switching of the load.
58 * - The range of the energy bucket has been reduced to one tenth of its former value. This
59 * allows the unit's operation to commence more rapidly whenever surplus power is available.
60 * - controlMode is no longer selectable, the unit's operation being effectively hard-coded
61 * as "Normal" rather than Anti-flicker.
62 * - Port D3 now supports an indicator which shows when the level in the energy bucket
63 * reaches either end of its range. While the unit is actively diverting surplus power,
64 * it is vital that the level in the reduced capacity energy bucket remains within its
65 * permitted range, hence the addition of this indicator.
66 *
67 * February 2020: updated to Mk2_fasterControl_1a with these changes:
68 * - removal of some redundant code in the logic for determining the next load state.
69 *
70 * March 2021: updated to Mk2_fasterControl_2 with these changes:
71 * - extra filtering added to offset the HPF effect of CT1. This allows the energy state in
72 * 10 ms time to be predicted with more confidence. Specifically, it is no longer necessary
73 * to include a 30% boost factor after each change of load state.
74 *
75 * June 2021: updated to Mk2_fasterControl_3 with these changes:

78 *
79 * July 2023: updated to Mk2_fasterControl_twoLoads_5 with these changes:
80 * - the ability to control two loads has been transferred from the latest version of my

82 * has been retained.
83 * The previous 2-load "faster control" sketch (version 4) has been archived as its
